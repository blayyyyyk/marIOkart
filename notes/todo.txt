So far, I've tried the following:
    i. Sweeping Rays:
        - Collect distance values of rays coming from the karts center of mass, going to first triangle intersection of the race course.
        - Rays are evenly distributed on the interval (0, pi) along the karts local xz-plane.
        - The number of rays $n_rays$ sampled is a hyperparameter of the model.
        - I've tried $n_rays$=3 and $n_rays$=24.
        - Choosing $n_rays$=3 caused the three rays the be place along the left, forward and right directions of the kart's local coordinate space.
        - Both cases for $n_rays$ results in loss of around 0.5-0.6.
    ii. Actions:
        - A keymask is a 11-bit binary value that represents the key states of the ds controller interface.
        - To give the model information about its prvious action in it feature vector, I map the previous keymask to an embedding space.
        - The number of embedding dimensions is a hyperparameter of the model.
        - The feature vector of the model is made by concatenating the sweeping ray distances and the embedding channels along the channel dimension.
        - There was no noticeable difference in the minimum loss but in training it took longer to reach the minimum loss I previously observed.
        - No noticeable differences in observations of the model's race evaluation.
    iii. Time Series:
        - I then made an LSTM make future keymask predictions based on previous feature vectors
        - 2d kernel (time, feature)
        - The following are new hyperparameters for the model:
            - Kernel Stride (every $s$ number of frames)
            - Kernel Size / Sequence length ($w$ number of frames)
            - Kernel Spacing ($x$ frames are between neighboring batches of frames
        - Adding time series resulted in loss of 0.3-0.4 (~30% improvement)
        - Training has plateaued at 0.3. I can't reach sub-.3 no matter what I choosing for kernel parameters
    iv. Sweeping Rays v2:
        - I tried to add the rates of change of the sweeping rays' distance features
        - I do this during data collection. (i.e. (dist(time=N)-dist(time=M))/(N-M))
        - The size of the observation vector is doubled as a result.
        - Learning slightly went up to ~0.4 during training. Accuracy went down 20%.
    v. Time Series v2:
        - Implementing a convolutional model, and testing it against the LSTM model
